# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

In the interest of fostering an open and welcoming environment, we as contributors and maintainers pledge to make participation in our project and our community a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, disability, ethnicity, gender identity and expression, level of experience, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

## Our Standards

### Behavior that contributes to a positive environment

- **Respectful Communication**: Use welcoming and inclusive language
- **Constructive Feedback**: Focus on what is best for the community
- **Empathy**: Show empathy towards other community members
- **Collaboration**: Work together to solve problems and create solutions
- **Mentorship**: Help newcomers learn and grow
- **Patience**: Understand that people have different levels of experience and availability

### Unacceptable behavior

- **Harassment**: Any form of harassment, sexual language, or imagery
- **Discrimination**: Discriminatory jokes, language, or actions
- **Personal Attacks**: Personal insults, trolling, or flaming
- **Spam**: Unwanted commercial content or self-promotion
- **Privacy Violations**: Publishing others' private information
- **Threats**: Threats of violence or any form of intimidation
- **Disruption**: Disrupting discussions, meetings, or events

## Expected Behaviors

### For Contributors

- **Code Reviews**: Provide constructive, respectful feedback
- **Issue Reporting**: Be thorough and respectful when reporting issues
- **Discussions**: Stay on topic and be open to different perspectives
- **Documentation**: Help improve documentation for all users

### For Maintainers

- **Fair Treatment**: Apply rules consistently to all community members
- **Clear Communication**: Provide clear explanations for decisions
- **Responsiveness**: Address issues and concerns in a timely manner
- **Mentorship**: Help contributors grow and learn

### For Community Members

- **Welcome Newcomers**: Help new contributors feel included
- **Ask Questions**: Encourage learning and curiosity
- **Share Knowledge**: Help others when you can
- **Report Issues**: Report violations to maintainers

## Enforcement Guidelines

### Reporting

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, please report it:

- **Email**: conduct@openrelief.org
- **Discord**: Message any maintainer directly
- **GitHub**: Report via [private issue](https://github.com/openrelief/openrelief/issues/new?template=conduct_report)

All reports will be reviewed and investigated within 7 business days.

### Consequences

Unacceptable behavior may result in:

- **Warning**: Private or public warning for minor violations
- **Temporary Suspension**: Temporary ban from community spaces
- **Permanent Ban**: Permanent removal from all community spaces
- **Project Removal**: Removal of commit access or maintainer status

### Appeal Process

If you believe a consequence is unfair:

1. Submit appeal to conduct@openrelief.org within 14 days
2. Include detailed explanation of the situation
3. Appeals will be reviewed by different maintainers
4. Decision will be communicated within 7 business days

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to all community spaces:

- **Online**: GitHub, Discord, forums, social media
- **Events**: Conferences, meetups, hackathons
- **Communication**: Emails, chats, video calls
- **Contributions**: Code, documentation, design, testing

## Specific Guidelines

### Communication Standards

#### Language
- Use inclusive language (e.g., "folks" instead of "guys")
- Avoid assumptions about gender, background, or experience
- Use clear, simple language when possible

#### Tone
- Assume good intent from others
- Be patient with questions and misunderstandings
- Focus on technical merit, not personal opinions

#### Feedback
- Critique code, not people
- Provide specific, actionable suggestions
- Acknowledge good work and improvements

### Code Reviews

#### For Reviewers
- **Be Constructive**: Focus on improving the code
- **Be Specific**: Provide clear, actionable feedback
- **Be Respectful**: Acknowledge the effort invested
- **Be Patient**: Understand different experience levels

#### For Authors
- **Be Open**: Accept feedback graciously
- **Be Responsive**: Address comments promptly
- **Be Thankful**: Appreciate the time reviewers invest
- **Be Clear**: Explain your reasoning when needed

### Issue Handling

#### For Reporters
- **Be Thorough**: Provide detailed information
- **Be Respectful**: Understand maintainers are volunteers
- **Be Patient**: Response times may vary
- **Be Helpful**: Provide additional information when requested

#### For Maintainers
- **Be Responsive**: Acknowledge issues promptly
- **Be Clear**: Communicate status and next steps
- **Be Fair**: Treat all users with respect
- **Be Helpful**: Guide users to solutions

## Diversity and Inclusion

### Commitment to Diversity

We are committed to creating an inclusive environment that welcomes people from all backgrounds:

- **Gender**: All gender identities and expressions
- **Race and Ethnicity**: All racial and ethnic backgrounds
- **Age**: People of all ages
- **Disability**: People with visible and invisible disabilities
- **Sexual Orientation**: All sexual orientations
- **Religion**: All religious beliefs and non-belief
- **Experience**: All levels of technical experience
- **Geography**: People from all geographic locations

### Inclusive Practices

- **Accessibility**: Ensure community spaces are accessible
- **Time Zones**: Schedule events across different time zones
- **Language**: Be patient with non-native English speakers
- **Cultural Awareness**: Respect different cultural practices
- **Economic Inclusion**: Remove barriers to participation

## Conflict Resolution

### Step 1: Direct Communication
- Try to resolve conflicts directly with the other person
- Assume good intent and misunderstandings
- Focus on the issue, not the person

### Step 2: Mediation
- If direct communication fails, request mediation
- Contact any maintainer for assistance
- Mediators will help facilitate constructive discussion

### Step 3: Formal Resolution
- If mediation fails, submit a formal report
- Follow the reporting process outlined above
- Maintainers will investigate and take appropriate action

## Enforcement Examples

### Minor Violations

**Example**: A contributor uses potentially exclusionary language in a comment.

**Response**:
1. Private message explaining the issue
2. Request to edit the comment
3. Public acknowledgment if corrected
4. Warning if behavior continues

### Major Violations

**Example**: A community member repeatedly harasses others in Discord.

**Response**:
1. Immediate temporary suspension from Discord
2. Investigation of all reported incidents
3. Permanent ban if harassment is confirmed
4. Communication to affected parties

### Extreme Violations

**Example**: Someone posts threatening content or personal information.

**Response**:
1. Immediate permanent ban from all community spaces
2. Removal of all content and contributions
3. Report to relevant authorities if necessary
4. Communication to community about safety measures
