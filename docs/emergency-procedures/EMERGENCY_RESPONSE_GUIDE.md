## Emergency Classification System

### Severity Levels

OpenRelief uses a standardized 5-level severity classification system to ensure appropriate response and resource allocation:

#### Level 1: Minor Incident
- **Description**: Limited impact, no immediate danger to life
- **Examples**: Small water leak, minor property damage, localized power outage
- **Response**: Community monitoring, low-priority notifications
- **Expected Response Time**: Within 24 hours

#### Level 2: Low Priority
- **Description**: Some concern, monitoring required but not urgent
- **Examples**: Fallen tree blocking minor road, small fire contained, minor injury
- **Response**: Community verification, local response coordination
- **Expected Response Time**: Within 12 hours

#### Level 3: Moderate Concern
- **Description**: Attention required, potential for escalation
- **Examples**: Building fire with evacuation, multiple injuries, flooding affecting several properties
- **Response**: Active community response, notification to wider area
- **Expected Response Time**: Within 6 hours

#### Level 4: Serious Situation
- **Description**: Urgent response needed, significant impact on community
- **Examples**: Major structure fire, widespread flooding, hazardous material spill
- **Response**: Immediate community mobilization, official service notification
- **Expected Response Time**: Within 2 hours

#### Level 5: Critical Emergency
- **Description**: Immediate danger to life, large-scale impact
- **Examples**: Multi-building fire, earthquake, tsunami warning, terrorist incident
- **Response**: Maximum alert, immediate official service involvement, mass notification
- **Expected Response Time**: Immediate - within minutes

### Emergency Types

#### Fire Emergencies
- **Code**: FIRE
- **Subtypes**: Building Fire, Wildfire, Vehicle Fire, Electrical Fire
- **Default Radius**: 500 meters
- **Priority Factors**: Population density, weather conditions, building type

#### Medical Emergencies
- **Code**: MEDICAL
- **Subtypes**: Injury, Cardiac Emergency, Overdose, Mass Casualty
- **Default Radius**: 300 meters
- **Priority Factors**: Number of victims, severity of injuries, access to medical facilities

#### Natural Disasters
- **Code**: NATURAL
- **Subtypes**: Flood, Earthquake, Storm, Landslide, Avalanche
- **Default Radius**: 1000 meters
- **Priority Factors**: Scale, speed of onset, population affected

#### Search and Rescue
- **Code**: SAR
- **Subtypes**: Missing Person, Trapped Individuals, Rescue Operation
- **Default Radius**: 800 meters
- **Priority Factors**: Time missing, weather conditions, terrain difficulty

#### Infrastructure Failures
- **Code**: INFRASTRUCTURE
- **Subtypes**: Building Collapse, Bridge Failure, Utility Failure, Road Blockage
- **Default Radius**: 600 meters
- **Priority Factors**: Essential services affected, number of people impacted

#### Transportation Accidents
- **Code**: ACCIDENT
- **Subtypes**: Vehicle Collision, Train Accident, Aviation Incident, Maritime Accident
- **Default Radius**: 400 meters
- **Priority Factors**: Number of vehicles involved, hazardous materials, injuries

#### Security Emergencies
- **Code**: SECURITY
- **Subtypes**: Active Threat, Civil Unrest, Terrorism, Hostage Situation
- **Default Radius**: 1500 meters
- **Priority Factors**: Threat level, number of potential victims, public accessibility

## Response Protocols by Emergency Type

### Fire Emergency Response

#### Immediate Actions (First 5 Minutes)

1. **Assess Situation Safely**
   - Maintain safe distance from fire
   - Identify fire type and size
   - Check for trapped individuals
   - Note wind direction and spread pattern

2. **Report Through OpenRelief**
   - Select "Fire" emergency type
   - Specify building type if known
   - Estimate number of people affected
   - Include access information for responders

3. **Personal Safety First**
   - Evacuate immediately if in danger zone
   - Help others evacuate only if safe to do so
   - Never re-enter burning buildings
   - Move to designated assembly points

#### Community Response (First 30 Minutes)

1. **Verification Process**
   - Confirm fire location and severity
   - Report any additional details
   - Update information as situation evolves
   - Coordinate with nearby community members

2. **Support Actions**
   - Clear access routes for emergency vehicles
   - Provide water sources if available
   - Assist with evacuation of vulnerable individuals
   - Share information about fire breaks and safety zones

3. **Information Management**
   - Avoid spreading unconfirmed information
   - Share official updates from fire department
   - Document evacuation routes and shelter locations
   - Monitor fire spread and weather conditions

#### Extended Response (First 2 Hours)

1. **Resource Coordination**
   - Identify available community resources
   - Coordinate temporary shelter needs
   - Organize volunteer assistance
   - Manage donation offers and needs

2. **Communication Hub**
   - Serve as information center for affected residents
   - Relay official announcements
   - Help reunite separated family members
   - Document damage assessments

#### Resolution Phase

1. **Recovery Support**
   - Coordinate cleanup efforts
   - Share information about assistance programs
   - Help with temporary housing needs
   - Support emotional recovery of affected individuals

2. **Documentation**
   - Update final status of emergency
   - Share lessons learned
   - Recognize community responders
   - Suggest improvements to response procedures

### Medical Emergency Response

#### Immediate Actions (First 5 Minutes)

1. **Scene Assessment**
   - Ensure scene is safe before approaching
   - Check number of victims and severity

3. **First Aid Intervention**

2. **Family Support**

#### Resolution Phase

2. **Prevention Education**
   - Share information about prevention

### Communication Best Practices

#### Clear and Concise Messaging

1. **Use Simple Language**
   - Avoid technical jargon
   - Use short, clear sentences
   - Provide specific, actionable information
   - Repeat critical information

2. **Include Essential Details**
   - What is happening
   - Where it is happening
   - Who is affected
   - What action to take

3. **Provide Context**
   - Explain urgency level
   - Give timeline for expected actions
   - Describe potential consequences
   - Include alternative options

#### Consistent Information Management

1. **Single Source of Truth**
   - Designate official information sources
   - Cross-reference information before sharing
   - Update information regularly
   - Correct misinformation quickly

2. **Verification Process**
   - Confirm information before broadcasting
   - Cite sources for shared information
   - Distinguish between confirmed and unconfirmed reports
   - Update as more information becomes available

#### Multilingual Communication

1. **Language Accessibility**
   - Provide information in multiple languages
   - Use translation services when available
   - Include visual aids and symbols
   - Consider literacy levels in messaging

2. **Cultural Considerations**
   - Adapt communication styles for different cultures
   - Consider cultural response patterns
   - Use appropriate terminology
   - Respect cultural communication norms

## Verification Procedures

### Verification System Overview

OpenRelief uses a multi-layered verification system to ensure the accuracy and reliability of emergency reports while maintaining rapid response capabilities.

### Verification Levels

#### Level 1: Automatic Verification
- **Triggers**: High-trust user reports, official service reports
- **Process**: Immediate acceptance with monitoring
- **Requirements**: User trust score >0.8, official source
- **Response**: Immediate notification to relevant area

#### Level 2: Community Verification
- **Triggers**: Standard user reports, moderate trust scores
- **Process**: Community confirmation required
- **Requirements**: Minimum 2 confirmations from different users
- **Response**: Notification after verification achieved

#### Level 3: Enhanced Verification
- **Triggers**: Low-trust users, sensitive emergency types
- **Process**: Multiple verification steps required
- **Requirements**: Photo/video evidence, multiple confirmations
- **Response**: Limited notification until enhanced verification

#### Level 4: Official Verification
- **Triggers**: Critical emergencies, large-scale events
- **Process**: Official service confirmation required
- **Requirements**: Emergency service verification
- **Response**: Maximum alert after official confirmation

### Verification Process

#### Step 1: Initial Report Assessment

1. **Report Analysis**
   - Check user trust score and history
   - Analyze report content for completeness
   - Evaluate location and emergency type
   - Assess severity level indicators

2. **Automated Checks**
   - Location validation
   - Emergency type classification
   - Duplicate report detection
   - Spam and fraud detection

3. **Initial Response Decision**
   - Assign verification level
   - Determine notification scope
   - Set response requirements
   - Establish monitoring parameters

#### Step 2: Community Verification

1. **Verification Requests**
   - Notify nearby users of new report
   - Request confirmation from users in area
   - Provide specific verification criteria
   - Set verification timeline

2. **Confirmation Process**
   - Users confirm if they can verify the emergency
   - Provide additional information if available
   - Rate confidence in verification
   - Submit supporting evidence

3. **Dispute Resolution**
   - Handle conflicting verifications
   - Investigate disputed reports
   - Consider user trust scores
   - Make verification decision

#### Step 3: Enhanced Verification

1. **Evidence Collection**
   - Request photo/video evidence
   - Ask for additional witnesses
   - Seek official confirmation
   - Document verification process

2. **Cross-Reference Checking**
   - Check for related reports
   - Verify with official sources
   - Analyze pattern consistency
   - Evaluate historical accuracy

3. **Final Verification Decision**
   - Make final verification determination
   - Update report status accordingly
   - Adjust user trust scores
   - Document verification outcome

### Verification Best Practices

#### For Verifiers

1. **Verify Only What You Know**
   - Confirm only emergencies you can directly verify
   - Be honest about your level of certainty
   - Provide specific details about what you observed
   - Avoid speculation or assumptions

2. **Provide Quality Information**
   - Include specific details in confirmations
   - Add relevant context about the situation
   - Note any changes since initial report
   - Share photos or videos if available and safe

3. **Maintain Objectivity**
   - Base verifications on facts, not emotions
   - Consider all available information
   - Avoid personal biases in verification
   - Focus on accuracy over speed

#### For Reporters

1. **Provide Detailed Information**
   - Include specific location details
   - Describe the situation clearly
   - Estimate severity accurately
   - Add photos/videos when safe

2. **Update Information Promptly**
   - Report changes in situation
   - Correct any inaccuracies
   - Add new information as available
   - Close reports when resolved

3. **Respond to Verification Requests**
   - Answer questions from verifiers
   - Provide additional details
   - Clarify any confusion
   - Cooperate with verification process

### Trust Score Impact

#### Positive Impacts

1. **Accurate Reporting**
   - +0.05 to +0.15 per verified accurate report
   - Higher increases for critical emergencies
   - Bonus for detailed, helpful reports
   - Additional points for photo/video evidence

2. **Helpful Verification**
   - +0.02 to +0.10 per helpful verification
   - Higher increases for confirming critical emergencies
   - Bonus for providing additional useful information
   - Recognition for consistent verification accuracy

3. **Community Contribution**
   - +0.01 to +0.05 for various helpful actions
   - Translation assistance for non-English reports
   - Helping coordinate community response
   - Supporting emergency response efforts

#### Negative Impacts

1. **Inaccurate Reporting**
   - -0.10 to -0.50 per false report
   - Larger decreases for serious false reports
   - Additional penalties for malicious false reports
   - Temporary suspension for repeated false reporting

2. **Poor Verification**
   - -0.05 to -0.20 per incorrect verification
   - Higher decreases for malicious verification
   - Additional penalties for verification abuse
   - Temporary verification privilege suspension

3. **Community Violations**
   - -0.10 to -0.30 for guideline violations
   - Higher penalties for serious violations
   - Account suspension for repeated violations
   - Permanent ban for severe violations

## Escalation and Resolution Workflows

### Escalation Procedures

#### Automatic Escalation Triggers

1. **Severity-Based Escalation**
   - Level 4+ emergencies automatically escalate
   - Multiple reports of same emergency escalate
   - High-trust user reports receive priority escalation
   - Official service reports trigger immediate escalation

2. **Time-Based Escalation**
   - Unverified Level 3 emergencies escalate after 15 minutes
   - Unverified Level 2 emergencies escalate after 30 minutes
   - Lack of response triggers escalation
   - Changing conditions trigger re-escalation

3. **Community Response Escalation**
   - High number of confirmations triggers escalation
   - Multiple dispute reports trigger review escalation
   - Community concern indicators trigger escalation
   - Resource shortage triggers escalation

#### Manual Escalation Process

1. **Community Escalation Request**
   - Users can request escalation of reports
   - Provide justification for escalation
   - Include additional supporting information
   - Review by moderators or high-trust users

2. **Moderator Escalation**
   - Designated moderators can escalate reports
   - Review all available information
   - Consider community input
   - Make escalation decision

3. **Official Service Escalation**
   - Emergency services can request escalation
   - Provide official assessment
   - Request specific response actions
   - Coordinate official response

   - Evacuate when in danger

2. **Scene Safety Assessment**

   - Identify ongoing dangers

   - Have backup plans

3. **Situational Awareness**
   - Stay aware of surroundings

#### Emergency-Specific Safety

#### Fire Safety

1. **Fire Response Safety**
   - Feel doors before opening
   - Stay low under smoke
   - Use stairs, not elevators
   - Close doors to contain fire

2. **Firefighter Safety**
   - Never re-enter burning buildings
   - Follow professional instructions
   - Maintain safe distance
   - Clear access for equipment

#### Medical Emergency Safety

1. **Scene Safety**
   - Ensure scene is safe before helping
   - Protect yourself from bodily fluids
   - Use protective equipment when available
   - Know your limits

2. **Medical Response Safety**
   - Call for professional help
   - Provide only trained assistance
   - Don't move injured unless necessary
   - Document provided care

1. **Weather Safety**

   - Stay away from downed power lines

   - Evacuate after shaking stops
