## Emergency Type Quick Reference

### Fire Emergency Quick Reference

```markdown
## ğŸ”¥ FIRE EMERGENCY QUICK REFERENCE

### IMMEDIATE ACTIONS
1. **EVACUATE** if fire is nearby or building is on fire
2. **CALL 911/112** from safe location
3. **CLOSE DOORS** to contain fire spread
4. **FEEL DOORS** before opening - hot means fire behind
5. **STAY LOW** under smoke to avoid inhalation
6. **NEVER USE ELEVATOR** during fire
7. **USE STAIRS** for evacuation

### REPORTING INFORMATION
#### Essential Details to Include
- **Location**: Exact address or GPS coordinates
- **Building Type**: House, apartment, office, etc.
- **Floor Number**: Which floor of building
- **Fire Size**: Small, medium, large
- **People Affected**: Number and location of people
- **Access Issues**: Blocked exits, special circumstances
- **Hazards**: Gas, chemicals, electrical, etc.

### FIRE TYPES & RESPONSE
| Type | Description | Immediate Action |
|-------|-------------|-----------------|
| Kitchen Fire | Cooking-related fire | Use fire extinguisher if small, evacuate if large |
| Electrical Fire | Wiring/appliance fire | Turn off power, evacuate if spreading |
| Wildfire | Outdoor vegetation fire | Evacuate immediately, follow official routes |
| Vehicle Fire | Car/truck fire | Move away, call fire department |
| Structure Fire | Building on fire | Evacuate, close doors, call from outside |

### SAFETY EQUIPMENT
- **Fire Extinguisher**: PASS method (Pull, Aim, Squeeze, Sweep)
- **Smoke Alarm**: Test monthly, replace batteries annually
- **Fire Escape Plan**: Know two ways out of every room
- **Meeting Place**: Designated safe meeting location outside
```

### Medical Emergency Quick Reference

```markdown
## ğŸ¥ MEDICAL EMERGENCY QUICK REFERENCE

### IMMEDIATE ACTIONS
1. **ASSESS SCENE** - Is it safe to approach?
2. **CALL 911/112** for serious medical emergencies
3. **PROVIDE CARE** only if trained and safe
4. **GATHER INFORMATION** for emergency responders
5. **STAY WITH PERSON** until help arrives

### REPORTING INFORMATION
#### Essential Details to Include
- **Type of Injury**: Bleeding, breathing, unconscious, etc.
- **Number of Victims**: How many people need help
- **Age of Victims**: Adult, child, elderly
- **Consciousness**: Alert, responsive, unconscious
- **Breathing**: Normal, difficulty, not breathing
- **Bleeding**: None, minor, moderate, severe
- **Pain Location**: Chest, abdomen, head, limbs
- **Known Conditions**: Diabetes, heart conditions, allergies
- **Medications** taken or needed

### MEDICAL EMERGENCY TYPES
| Type | Symptoms | Immediate Action |
|-------|-----------|-----------------|
| Heart Attack | Chest pain, shortness of breath | Call 911, give aspirin if available |
| Stroke | Face drooping, arm weakness, speech difficulty | Call 911, note time of onset |
| Choking | Cannot speak, breathe, or cough | Perform Heimlich maneuver or back blows |
| Seizure | Convulsions, loss of consciousness | Protect from injury, time seizure, call 911 |
| Bleeding | Significant blood loss | Apply direct pressure, call 911 |
| Breathing Difficulty | Shortness of breath, wheezing | Call 911, help person sit upright |
| Unconsciousness | Unresponsive, not breathing | Call 911, start CPR if trained |

### FIRST AID BASICS
#### CPR (Cardiopulmonary Resuscitation)
- **C**: Compressions - 30 compressions at 100-120/min
- **A**: Airway - Head tilt, chin lift
- **B**: Breathing - 2 rescue breaths
- **D**: Defibrillation - Use AED if available
- **Ratio**: 30:2 for one rescuer, 15:2 for two rescuers

#### BLEEDING CONTROL
- **Direct Pressure**: Apply firm pressure to wound
- **Elevation**: Raise injured limb above heart level
- **Pressure Points**: Apply pressure to artery above wound
- **Tourniquet**: Last resort for severe limb bleeding
```

### Natural Disaster Quick Reference

```markdown
## ğŸŒŠ NATURAL DISASTER QUICK REFERENCE

### EARTHQUAKE ACTIONS
#### During Shaking
1. **DROP, COVER, HOLD ON**
   - Drop to hands and knees
   - Take cover under table/desk
   - Hold on until shaking stops

#### After Shaking
1. **CHECK FOR INJURIES**
2. **EVACUATE IF NEEDED**
3. **AVOID DAMAGED BUILDINGS**
4. **CHECK UTILITIES** - Gas, water, electrical
5. **LISTEN FOR UPDATES** from emergency services

### FLOOD ACTIONS
#### Before Flood
1. **MOVE TO HIGHER GROUND**
2. **TURN OFF UTILITIES** at main breakers
3. **GATHER EMERGENCY SUPPLIES**
4. **PLAN EVACUATION ROUTES**

#### During Flood
1. **NEVER WALK IN FLOODING WATER**
2. **AVOID DOWNED POWER LINES**
3. **MOVE TO UPPER FLOORS** if water rises
4. **USE BOAT** only if trained
5. **STAY INFORMED** via radio/phone

### HURRICANE/TORNADO ACTIONS
#### Before Storm
1. **SECURE OUTDOOR OBJECTS**
2. **BOARD UP WINDOWS**
3. **GATHER SUPPLIES**
4. **KNOW EVACUATION ROUTES**
5. **IDENTIFY SAFE ROOM**

#### During Storm
1. **SHELTER IN PLACE** or evacuate as ordered
2. **STAY AWAY FROM WINDOWS**
3. **MONITOR WEATHER UPDATES**
4. **USE FLASHLIGHTS** if power fails
5. **HAVE BATTERY RADIO** for information
```

### Security Emergency Quick Reference

```markdown
## ğŸš¨ SECURITY EMERGENCY QUICK REFERENCE

### ACTIVE THREAT SITUATIONS
#### Active Shooter/Violent Intruder
1. **RUN** - Escape if possible
2. **HIDE** - If cannot escape safely
3. **FIGHT** - As last resort, only if life in danger

#### RUN - ESCAPE PLAN
1. **HAVE ESCAPE ROUTES** planned
2. **LEAVE BELONGINGS** behind
3. **HELP OTHERS** escape if possible
4. **CALL 911** when safe
5. **DO NOT STOP** until in safe location

#### HIDE - SHELTER PLAN
1. **LOCK/BARRICADE** the room
2. **SILENCE PHONE** - turn on vibrate
3. **HIDE** out of sight
4. **REMAIN QUIET** - no talking
5. **WAIT FOR HELP** - police will clear rooms

#### FIGHT - LAST RESORT
1. **COMMIT TO ACTIONS** - decide to survive
2. **IMPROVISE WEAPONS** - use available objects
3. **TARGET VULNERABILITIES** - eyes, throat, groin
4. **CREATE DISTRACTION** - throw objects, set off alarms
5. **FIGHT UNTIL HELP ARRIVES** - do not stop

### REPORTING INFORMATION
#### Essential Details for Security Emergencies
- **Threat Type**: Active shooter, intruder, violence
- **Location**: Building, floor, room number
- **Suspect Description**: Height, clothing, weapons
- **Direction of Movement**: Where threat is heading
- **Number of Threats**: Single or multiple
- **People in Danger**: Who and where
- **Safe Escape Routes**: Known exits and paths
- **Shelter Locations**: Safe rooms or assembly points
```

### Home Safety Quick Reference

```markdown
## ğŸ  HOME SAFETY QUICK REFERENCE

### HOME EMERGENCY KIT
#### Essential Items
- **Water**: 1 gallon per person per day (3-day supply)
- **Food**: 3-day supply of non-perishable food
- **Medications**: 7-day supply, prescription and over-the-counter
- **First Aid**: Bandages, antiseptic, pain relievers, etc.
- **Tools**: Flashlight, batteries, radio, whistle
- **Documents**: IDs, insurance policies, bank records
- **Cash**: Small bills and coins for emergencies

### HOME SECURITY MEASURES
#### Doors and Windows
- **Deadbolts**: Use on all exterior doors
- **Peepholes**: Install and use before opening doors
- **Window Locks**: Secure all windows when away or sleeping
- **Reinforcement**: Security film on ground floor windows
- **Sliding Doors**: Place security bar in track

#### Lighting
- **Exterior Lighting**: Motion-activated floodlights
- **Interior Timers**: Randomize lighting schedules
- **Emergency Lighting**: Battery-powered flashlights
- **Exit Lighting**: Marked exit paths with illumination

### UTILITY SAFETY
#### Gas Safety
- **Carbon Monoxide**: Install detectors, test monthly
- **Gas Leaks**: Know smell, leave immediately, call from outside
- **Appliance Connections**: Check regularly for leaks
- **Professional Inspection**: Annual inspection of gas systems

#### Electrical Safety
- **Circuit Breakers**: Know location and how to reset
- **Power Outages**: Flashlights, battery radio, non-cordless phone
- **Surge Protection**: Power strips with surge protection
- **Cord Management**: Remove damaged cords, don't overload outlets
```

## First Aid Quick Reference

### Medical First Aid Quick Reference

```markdown
## ğŸ¥ FIRST AID QUICK REFERENCE

### ASSESSMENT FIRST
#### DR. ABCDE Approach
- **D** - Danger: Check for ongoing dangers
- **R** - Response: Is the person responsive?
- **A** - Airway: Is the airway clear?
- **B** - Breathing: Is the person breathing?
- **C** - Circulation: Is there a pulse?
- **D** - Disability: Any neurological deficits?
- **E** - Exposure: Protect from environment

### CONSCIOUSNESS LEVELS
#### AVPU Scale
| Level | Description | Action |
|--------|-------------|--------|
| A | Alert | Person is awake and responsive |
| V | Verbal | Responds to voice, not fully alert |
| P | Pain | Responds to pain stimuli |
| U | Unresponsive | No response to any stimuli |

### BLEEDING CONTROL
#### Direct Pressure Method
1. **Apply Pressure**: Use clean cloth or gloved hand
2. **Elevate**: Raise injured area above heart level
3. **Pressure Points**: Apply pressure to artery above wound
4. **Maintain Pressure**: Continue until bleeding stops or help arrives
5. **Do Not Remove**: Don't remove cloths that are soaked with blood

#### Tourniquet Use (Last Resort)
1. **Apply 2-3 inches** above wound
2. **Use Wide Material**: At least 2 inches wide
3. **Tighten Until Bleeding Stops**
4. **Note Time**: Write application time on person
5. **Medical Attention**: Tourniquets can cause tissue damage

### BURN TREATMENT
#### Minor Burns (First Degree)
1. **Cool Water**: Run cool (not cold) water for 10-15 minutes
2. **Remove Jewelry**: From burned area
3. **Cover Loosely**: With sterile, non-stick dressing
4. **Pain Relief**: Over-the-counter pain relievers
5. **Monitor for Infection**: Watch for increased redness, swelling

#### Major Burns (Second/Third Degree)
1. **DO NOT Apply Water** - Can cause shock
2. **Cover with Clean Cloth** - Prevent contamination
3. **Treat for Shock** - Keep person warm
4. **Seek Immediate Medical Care** - Call 911/112
5. **Do Not Break Blisters** - Increases infection risk

### FRACTURE/SUSPECTED INJURY
#### Immobilization
1. **Don't Move** Unless person is in danger
2. **Support Above and Below** Injury site
3. **Apply Splint** If trained, use available materials
4. **Check Circulation** Before and after splinting
5. **Treat for Shock** - Keep person warm and calm

### SEVERE ALLERGIC REACTION
#### Anaphylaxis Response
1. **Epinephrine** If available and person has auto-injector
2. **Call 911/112** Immediately
3. **Airway Support** Person may need help breathing
4. **Lie Down Flat** With legs elevated
5. **Stay Calm** Anxiety can worsen reaction
```

### Choking/Heimlich Maneuver Quick Reference

```markdown
## ğŸ˜· CHOKING QUICK REFERENCE

### RECOGNITION SIGNS
| Sign | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| Cannot Speak, Cough, or Cry | Airway blocked |
| Hands Clutching Throat | Universal choking sign |
| Blue Lips/Face | Lack of oxygen |
| Panic Expression | Person recognizes danger |
| No Breathing Sounds | Complete airway obstruction |

### HEIMLICH MANEUVER
#### Standing Position (Conscious Person)
1. **Stand Behind** Person
2. **Wrap Arms** Around waist
3. **Make Fist** with one hand
4. **Place Fist** Above navel, below rib cage
5. **Thrust Upward and Inward** Quick, forceful movement
6. **Repeat** Until object expelled or person becomes unconscious

#### Chest Thrusts (Unconscious/Obese Person)
1. **Place Person** On their back
2. **Kneel** Above their head
3. **Place Heels** of hands on center of breastbone
4. **Deliver 5 Quick Thrusts** Downward
5. **Check Mouth** For expelled object
6. **Start CPR** If breathing doesn't resume

### INFANT CHOKING (Under 1 Year)
#### Back Blows and Chest Thrusts
1. **Hold Infant** Face down on your forearm
2. **Support Head** With your other hand
3. **Deliver 5 Back Blows** Between shoulder blades
4. **Turn Infant Over** Support head and neck
5. **Deliver 5 Chest Thrusts** Using two fingers
6. **Check Mouth** Remove object if visible
7. **Call 911** If object doesn't come out
```

## Evacuation Quick Reference

### Evacuation Planning Quick Reference

```markdown
## ğŸš¶ EVACUATION QUICK REFERENCE

### EVACUATION BASICS
#### The 3 P's of Evacuation
1. **PLAN** - Know evacuation routes and meeting places
2. **PREPARE** - Have emergency kit and supplies ready
3. **PRACTICE** - Regular evacuation drills with family

#### EVACUATION TRIGGERS
| Trigger | Action | Time Frame |
|---------|--------|------------|
| Official Order | Evacuate immediately | Immediately |
| Immediate Danger | Evacuate without waiting | Immediately |
| Warning Issued | Prepare to evacuate | 30 minutes to 2 hours |
| Weather Alert | Monitor and prepare | 2-24 hours notice |

### EVACUATION ROUTES
#### Primary and Secondary Routes
- **Two Ways Out** of every room
- **Avoid Elevators** during fires/earthquakes
- **Know Window Exits** as alternative to doors
- **Consider Mobility Needs** of all family members
- **Practice Routes** in daylight and darkness

### MEETING PLACES
#### Designated Safe Locations
- **Outdoor Meeting Place**: Away from buildings, trees, power lines
- **Indoor Shelter**: Basement, interior room, away from windows
- **Out-of-Area Contact**: Person outside danger zone to relay messages
- **Multiple Meeting Places**: Primary, secondary, tertiary options

### SPECIAL CONSIDERATIONS
#### Mobility Needs
- **Wheelchair Accessible Routes**
- **Assistance Equipment** needed
- **Extra Time** for evacuation
- **Service Animals** accommodation

#### Children and Elderly
- **Practice Together** regularly
- **Buddy System** pair vulnerable with able person
- **Extra Time** for preparation and evacuation
- **Comfort Items** for stress reduction
```
