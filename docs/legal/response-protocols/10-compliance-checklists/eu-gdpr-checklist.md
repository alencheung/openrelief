# EU GDPR Compliance Checklist

## Overview

This checklist provides comprehensive compliance requirements for OpenRelief's handling of government data requests under the EU General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR). The checklist is designed to ensure GDPR compliance while responding to legitimate legal requests.

## Legal Framework Summary

### Key GDPR Provisions Relevant to Government Requests

#### Article 6: Lawfulness of Processing
- **Point (e)**: Processing necessary for compliance with legal obligation
- **Point (d)**: Processing necessary to protect vital interests of data subject
- **Point (c)**: Processing necessary for compliance with legal obligation to which controller is subject

#### Article 9: Processing of Special Categories of Data
- **Point (g)**: Processing necessary for reasons of substantial public interest
- **Point (h)**: Processing necessary for archiving purposes in public interest, scientific or historical research purposes or statistical purposes

#### Article 15: Right of Access by Data Subject
- Right to obtain confirmation of processing
- Right to access personal data
- Right to information about processing purposes

#### Article 16: Right to Rectification
- Right to rectification of inaccurate personal data
- Right to completion of incomplete personal data

#### Article 17: Right to Erasure ('Right to be Forgotten')
- Right to erasure of personal data when no longer necessary
- Right to erasure when consent withdrawn
- Right to erasure when unlawfully processed

#### Article 18: Right to Restriction of Processing
- Right to restriction of processing in certain circumstances
- Right to notification when restriction is lifted

#### Article 19: Right to Data Portability
- Right to receive personal data in structured, commonly used format
- Right to transmit data to another controller

#### Article 21: Right to Object
- Right to object to processing of personal data
- Right to object to direct marketing

#### Article 25: Data Protection by Design and by Default
- Implementation of appropriate technical and organizational measures
- Privacy by design principles
- Data minimization by default

#### Article 32: Security of Processing
- Appropriate technical and organizational security measures
- Risk-based approach to security
- Regular testing, assessment and evaluation

#### Article 33: Notification of Personal Data Breach
- Notification to supervisory authority within 72 hours
- Notification to data subjects without undue delay
- Documentation of personal data breaches

#### Article 35: Data Protection Impact Assessment (DPIA)
- Required for high-risk processing
- Systematic description of processing operations
- Assessment of necessity and proportionality

#### Article 36: Prior Consultation
- Consultation with supervisory authority prior to processing
- Required when DPIA indicates high risk

## Compliance Checklist

### 1. Legal Basis Assessment

#### ✅ Legal Basis Verification
- [ ] Legal obligation clearly identified and documented
- [ ] Legal basis is valid under EU law
- [ ] Legal basis is specific and not overly broad
- [ ] Legal basis is proportionate to the aim pursued
- [ ] Legal basis has proper legal foundation

#### ✅ Vital Interests Assessment
- [ ] Vital interests of data subject identified
- [ ] Vital interests are life-threatening or serious injury
- [ ] Processing is strictly necessary to protect vital interests
- [ ] No less intrusive means available
- [ ] Processing is proportionate to the threat

#### ✅ Substantial Public Interest Assessment
- [ ] Substantial public interest clearly identified
- [ ] Interest is recognized by EU law
- [ ] Processing is necessary for the public interest
- [ ] Processing is proportionate to the public interest
- [ ] Appropriate safeguards are in place

### 2. Data Subject Rights Implementation

#### ✅ Right to Access Implementation
- [ ] Data subject can access their personal data
- [ ] Access requests processed within one month
- [ ] Access provided in commonly used electronic format
- [ ] Information about processing purposes provided
- [ ] Information about recipients of personal data provided

#### ✅ Right to Rectification Implementation
- [ ] Data subject can rectify inaccurate personal data
- [ ] Rectification requests processed within one month
- [ ] Incomplete personal data can be completed
- [ ] Third parties notified of rectification where necessary
- [ ] Rectification confirmed to data subject

#### ✅ Right to Erasure Implementation
- [ ] Data subject can request erasure of personal data
- [ ] Erasure requests processed within one month
- [ ] Erasure includes all copies and backups
- [ ] Third parties notified of erasure where necessary
- [ ] Erasure confirmed to data subject

#### ✅ Right to Restriction Implementation
- [ ] Data subject can request restriction of processing
- [ ] Restriction requests processed within one month
- [ ] Restricted data is clearly identified
- [ ] Processing is only performed with data subject consent
- [ ] Data subject notified when restriction is lifted

#### ✅ Right to Data Portability Implementation
- [ ] Data subject can receive personal data in structured format
- [ ] Data provided in machine-readable format
- [ ] Data can be transmitted to another controller
- [ ] Portability requests processed within one month
- [ ] No adverse effects on data subject rights

#### ✅ Right to Object Implementation
- [ ] Data subject can object to processing of personal data
- [ ] Objection requests processed within one month
- [ ] Processing stops upon objection unless legitimate grounds exist
- [ ] Data subject informed of grounds for continuing processing
- [ ] Right to object explained clearly to data subjects

### 3. Data Protection by Design and Default

#### ✅ Privacy by Design Implementation
- [ ] Privacy considerations integrated from system design
- [ ] DPIA conducted for high-risk processing
- [ ] Privacy impact assessments documented
- [ ] Privacy-enhancing technologies implemented
- [ ] Regular privacy reviews conducted

#### ✅ Data Minimization Implementation
- [ ] Only necessary personal data collected
- [ ] Data collection limited to specified purposes
- [ ] Data retention periods established and enforced
- [ ] Unnecessary personal data regularly deleted
- [ ] Data minimization documented and justified

#### ✅ Purpose Limitation Implementation
- [ ] Personal data processed for specified purposes only
- [ ] No further processing incompatible with original purposes
- [ ] Data subjects informed of processing purposes
- [ ] Changes to purposes require new legal basis
- [ ] Purpose limitation documented and monitored

### 4. Security Measures

#### ✅ Technical Security Measures
- [ ] Encryption of personal data at rest
- [ ] Encryption of personal data in transit
- [ ] Pseudonymization of personal data where appropriate
- [ ] Access controls based on need-to-know principle
- [ ] Regular security testing and assessment

#### ✅ Organizational Security Measures
- [ ] Data protection policies and procedures implemented
- [ ] Staff training on data protection implemented
- [ ] Data protection officer appointed
- [ ] Data protection by design principles embedded in culture
- [ ] Regular security awareness training conducted

#### ✅ Incident Response Procedures
- [ ] Personal data breach detection procedures implemented
- [ ] Breach notification procedures established
- [ ] 72-hour notification to supervisory authority capability
- [ ] Data subject notification procedures established
- [ ] Breach documentation and analysis procedures

### 5. Government Request Specific Procedures

#### ✅ Request Verification Procedures
- [ ] Government request authenticity verified
- [ ] Requesting authority legal competence verified
- [ ] Request scope and specificity assessed
- [ ] Legal basis for request verified
- [ ] Jurisdictional authority confirmed

#### ✅ Data Minimization for Government Requests
- [ ] Only data specifically requested is disclosed
- [ ] Data scope limited to legal minimum
- [ ] Precision reduction applied where possible
- [ ] Aggregation used instead of individual data where possible
- [ ] Anonymization applied where disclosure not essential

#### ✅ User Notification Procedures
- [ ] User notification procedures established
- [ ] Legal restrictions on notification assessed
- [ ] Notification timing determined by legal requirements
- [ ] Notification content includes all required information
- [ ] User rights information provided with notification

#### ✅ Legal Challenge Procedures
- [ ] Government request challenge procedures established
- [ ] Legal review process for all requests
- [ ] Challenge criteria and decision trees developed
- [ ] External counsel engagement procedures
- [ ] User support for legal challenges provided

### 6. Cross-Border Data Transfers

#### ✅ Transfer Mechanism Verification
- [ ] Adequacy decision for destination country verified
- [ ] Standard contractual clauses implemented where required
- [ ] Binding corporate rules implemented where appropriate
- [ ] Specific derogations assessed and documented
- [ ] Transfer impact assessments conducted

#### ✅ Transfer Safeguards Implementation
- [ ] Appropriate safeguards for each transfer implemented
- [ ] Data subject rights maintained after transfer
- [ ] Transfer documentation completed and maintained
- [ ] Regular review of transfer mechanisms
- [ ] Supervisory authority notified where required

### 7. Documentation and Record-Keeping

#### ✅ Processing Records
- [ ] Records of processing activities maintained
- [ ] Records include all required information
- [ ] Records are accurate and up-to-date
- [ ] Records are accessible to supervisory authority
- [ ] Records retention period established

#### ✅ Legal Request Documentation
- [ ] All government requests documented
- [ ] Request processing decisions documented
- [ ] Legal basis for decisions documented
- [ ] Data disclosed documented
- [ ] User notifications documented

#### ✅ DPIA Documentation
- [ ] DPIA conducted for high-risk processing
- [ ] DPIA methodology documented
- [ ] Risk assessment results documented
- [ ] Mitigation measures documented
- [ ] Supervisory authority consultation documented
